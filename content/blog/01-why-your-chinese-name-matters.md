You've seen it. Someone walks into a tattoo parlor, points at a random Chinese character on the wall, and walks out with "chicken soup" permanently inked on their arm. Or a business executive hands out name cards in Shanghai with a Chinese name that translates to something... unfortunate.

**Getting a Chinese name isn't about picking pretty characters. It's about getting it right.**

## The Problem: Phonetic Translation Ruins Meaning

Most Chinese name generators do one thing: they take the sounds of your English name and find Chinese characters that sound similar. That's how "Michael" becomes 迈克尔 (Mài Kè Ěr) — three characters that together mean "stride, overcome, you." Not terrible. But not meaningful either.

The real question is: **what story does your name tell?**

## What Makes a Chinese Name Meaningful

A great Chinese name connects three things:

1. **Your identity** — What your English name actually means, not just how it sounds. "Sophia" means wisdom. A good Chinese name should carry that meaning.

2. **Cultural roots** — The best Chinese names reference classical poetry and philosophy. When your name contains a character that appears in a Tang Dynasty poem, you're not just getting a label. You're getting 1,300 years of cultural heritage.

3. **Elemental balance** — In traditional Chinese naming, your birth date determines your Five Elements profile. The right name balances what you're missing. Too much Fire? Add Water characters. Missing Metal? Your name should carry it.
